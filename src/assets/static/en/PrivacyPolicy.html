How We Respond to “Do Not Track” Signals

Some web browsers provide Do Not Track ("DNT") signals, which tell a web application to stop tracking a specific user. When you enable the DNT setting in your browser, it sends a specific signal to websites, analytics companies, ad networks, plug-in providers, and/or other web services that you visit to tell them to stop tracking your browsing activities. There is no agreed-upon definition of "Do Not Track" in this situation among industry participants. We may not change our practices when our systems receive a "Do Not Track" signal from a visitor's browser, as many other websites and online services do, but we will if required by law.
